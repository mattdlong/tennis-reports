### Variance Drivers

**High Break Rate Volatility:**
Both players have 42-44% break rates, meaning approximately 4-5 service breaks per match are expected. The sequence and timing of these breaks creates high variance in both total games and game margin. A 6-3, 6-3 result (18 games, Stakusic -6) versus a 7-5, 6-4 result (22 games, Stakusic -3) dramatically affects both markets despite similar competitive dynamics.

**Tiebreak Uncertainty:**
Hon has 20% probability of >0.5 tiebreaks per match with 66.7% win rate (n=10 TBs on hard court). Stakusic has limited tiebreak data (n=6 overall). A single tiebreak adds 2 games minimum to the total and can swing spread by ±1 game. Low TB probability is baked into model but small sample sizes create uncertainty.

**Form vs Quality Divergence:**
Stakusic's current form (3-1 in 2026, 3-match win streak, dominant qualifying) contradicts Hon's superior 2025 hard court record (69% vs 62.5%). Model uncertainty exists around which metric is more predictive. If Hon finds her 2025 form, she could win comfortably. If Stakusic maintains qualifying momentum, she could dominate.

**Straight Sets Impact:**
Model projects 65% probability of straight sets. If correct, total lands in 18-21 range. However, Hon wins 57% of her victories in 3 sets. A competitive 3-setter pushes total to 23-26 games, completely changing the distribution.
