## Risk & Unknowns

### Variance Drivers

- **Extreme Heat Impact:** 100°F+ temperatures create high retirement risk. If either player retires, total games could be dramatically lower (sub-30). Heat also affects service efficiency unpredictably - some players' serve deteriorates more than others under heat stress.

- **Best of 5 Format Variance:** Bo5 creates wider outcome distribution than Bo3. While 3-1 is most likely (48%), there's still 27% chance of five-setter which would push total toward or over the line.

- **Tiebreak Volatility:** With 62% probability of at least 1 tiebreak, variance is introduced. If multiple tiebreaks occur (35% probability of 2+), total could increase by 2-4 games. Tiebreak outcomes are essentially coin flips that add high-variance games.

- **Form Divergence Uncertainty:** Halys' 2026 sample shows 100% straight-set results (avg 18.3 games in Bo3). Tabilo's 2026 sample shows 100% three-set results (avg 29.3 games in Bo3). These patterns are from small samples and may not reflect true skill - they could be variance or opponent quality effects.

- **Hold Rate Sample Size:** Hold/break percentages are from last 52 hard court matches, which is reasonable sample but still subject to ~2-3% uncertainty. Small changes in hold rates can affect expected total by 2-3 games in Bo5.

### Data Limitations

- **Tiebreak Statistics:** Tiebreak frequency and win rates are estimated based on typical patterns for players with these hold rates. Actual TB statistics not available in granular detail, introducing uncertainty in TB probability modeling.

- **No Hard Court H2H:** First hard court meeting between these players. Grass H2H from 2022 is irrelevant for hard court totals/spread prediction.

- **Limited 2026 Data:** Both players have only 3 matches recorded in 2026. Recent form patterns may be noise rather than signal. Halys' straight-set pattern and Tabilo's three-set pattern could both be small-sample variance.

- **Extreme Conditions Impact Unknown:** While we know 100°F+ heat is factor, we don't have historical data on how these specific players perform in extreme heat. Some players handle heat better than others.

- **Second Week Scheduling:** As first round, both players relatively fresh. But extreme heat on Day 1-2 of Grand Slam can be unpredictable for player stamina, especially if match scheduled in peak heat hours.

### Correlation Notes

- **Totals and Spread Correlation:** Under total and Tabilo spread covering are POSITIVELY correlated. If Tabilo dominates (3-0 or 3-1), both Under and Tabilo -2.5 hit. If match goes five sets, both lose. Combined position of 2.2 units total is acceptable given correlation works in our favor.

- **Retirement Correlation:** If either player retires due to heat, both Under and Tabilo -2.5 likely hit (assuming Tabilo is the one who survives or dominates before retirement). This positive correlation actually reduces combined risk.

- **Market Liquidity:** Grand Slam first round typically has decent liquidity on totals. Spread markets may be limited or higher vig. Prioritize totals position if forced to choose.
